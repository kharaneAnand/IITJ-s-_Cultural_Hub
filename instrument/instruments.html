Instrument of the Month

Ravanhatta

The Ravanhatta is a bowed string instrument, believed to have originated in Rajasthan, India, and is one of the oldest known instruments in Indian folk music. It is traditionally associated with the Bhopa community and is said to have been played by King Ravana of Lanka, from whom it derives its name. The instrument consists of a long bamboo neck, a resonator made from a coconut shell, and two main playing strings, along with sympathetic strings for resonance. Today, it remains an important part of folk traditions in Rajasthan and Gujarat.
